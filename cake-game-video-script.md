# Cake Game — 30-Second Video Script

**Audience:** Early-career professionals / students new to supply chain  
**Format:** Slides / animation + voiceover  
**Tone:** Storytelling / narrative  
**Runtime:** ~30 seconds (~80 words spoken at a comfortable pace)

---

## Voiceover Script

> Imagine a bakery. Cakes are baked, packed, shipped, and sold — through four hands:
> **Manufacturer, Distributor, Wholesaler, Retailer.**
>
> Every week, each player orders from the one upstream. Simple enough.
>
> But here's the catch — **your order takes time to arrive.** And cakes **expire in 3 weeks.**
>
> Order too little? Lost sales. Order too much? Wastage.
>
> The twist: **information travels slower than you think.** By the time demand reaches the manufacturer, it's already old news.
>
> Can your team keep costs low — together?
>
> **This is the Cake Game.**

## Key concepts to land (in 30 seconds)

1. **Material flow is slow** — physical goods move upstream to downstream over multiple weeks
2. **Information flow is even slower** — a demand spike at Retail takes weeks to reach Manufacturing
3. **Perishability creates urgency** — you cannot sit on inventory; 3-week shelf life forces discipline
4. **Cost trade-offs are real** — every decision has a price: lost sales ($4) · wastage ($2) · holding ($0.50/week)
5. **It's a team game** — your decisions affect everyone else in the chain

---

## Notes for the animator / editor

- Keep visuals simple and fast-moving — this is a teaser, not a tutorial
- The four supply chain boxes should flow left-to-right (Manufacturer → Retailer), mirroring how cakes move
- Show order arrows going **right-to-left** (Retailer → Manufacturer) to contrast with cake flow
- Use warm cake colours (browns, ambers, creams) consistent with the game's UI palette
- End frame should show the game URL / QR code if used for recruitment/onboarding
